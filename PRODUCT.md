# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary audience for this soft-launch surface: pre-seed investors evaluating Hardwire. Secondary: defense / industrial operators and robotics OEMs who may see the same artifact in diligence. Context is a high-signal first impression, not a product walkthrough. They need to grasp category, wedge, and seriousness in seconds.

## Product Purpose

Hardwire is physical AI infrastructure: the deployment layer that turns robotics demos into trusted field outcomes. Hardwire does not build robot bodies. It assembles robots, sensors, models, power, comms, and compute into paid deployments with evidence attached (disconnected operations, infrastructure rounds, reliability & mission assurance). Tagline locked for this surface: "The harness for physical, real-world AI." Mission line from the formation deck: American intelligence in American machines.

## Brand Personality

Hard-tech. Reindustrialize. Mission-grade.

Voice should feel closer to Anduril / Palantir / defense-industrial systems than consumer SaaS: sparse, exact, evidence-first, zero fluff. Industrial amber on black. Pixel + mono instrumentation typography. The page should read like a deployment HUD, not a startup brochure.

## Anti-references

- Soft SaaS marketing (purple gradients, rounded card grids, friendly illustrations)
- Neon cyberpunk / Matrix cosplay
- Editorial magazine / Klim-specimen landing aesthetics
- "AI wrapper" consumer product pages
- Playful startup whimsy, emojis, pill clusters, stat strips in the hero

## Design Principles

1. **Deployment layer, not demos** — Visual hierarchy should make Hardwire the center that aggregates inputs (comms, sensors, robots, compute, power, models), not a feature grid of equal cards.
2. **Evidence over hype** — Tight type, hard edges, no decorative chrome. Every element should feel operational.
3. **Industrial amber as identity** — Commit to the orange drench on the brand bar; keep the stage black and instrumental.
4. **HUD density** — Prefer tight groupings and precise spacing over airy marketing whitespace. Investor surfaces should feel serious and packed with intent.
5. **American machines** — Sharp Motorblock wordmark, geometric emblem, no softened consumer brand cues.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast on text (white on near-black cards; black on amber footer). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for globe rotation and ripple. Touch targets on mobile cards should remain usable even when visually compact.
